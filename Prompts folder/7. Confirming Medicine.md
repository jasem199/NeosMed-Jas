### Confirming Medicine Taken When Alarm Triggers - Flow Structure

A home screen centered daily medicine tracking flow with a date-based navigation system. Medicines are grouped by time category and users can confirm, snooze, skip or review individual medicines. Designed to be operable in seconds, minimal interaction required.

---

### Entry Point

**Home Screen** — This IS the home screen. No navigation required.

---

### Step 1 — Date Picker (Top Section)

- Horizontal scrollable date strip at the top
- Current date highlighted in primary color
- Tapped/selected date highlighted in secondary color
- Dates show day name and date number (Mon 4)
- Small **"Today"** button appears on the right only when user is viewing a different date
- Tapping "Today" snaps back to current date with a smooth scroll animation
- Icon: Calendar icon on far left to open a full month picker if needed

**Reasoning:** A horizontal date strip is the most thumb-friendly date navigation for mobile. Showing day name with date number helps aged users orient themselves without confusion. The "Today" button prevents users from getting lost in past dates.

---

### Step 2 — Medicine Schedule (Main Canvas)

- Full scrollable canvas below the date strip
- Medicines grouped by time category:
    - 🌅 Morning
    - ☀️ Afternoon
    - 🌙 Evening
    - 🌑 Night
- Each category has a section header with time label and a **"Check All"** button on the right
- Medicine card contains:
    - Medicine name (large, readable font)
    - Dosage and unit (e.g. 1 pill, 2 drops)
    - Intake advice tag (Before meal / After meal / With meal)
    - A large circular **Check button** on the right side of the card

**Reasoning:** Grouping by time reduces cognitive load — users only need to focus on the relevant time group at a glance. Large check button on the right is thumb-friendly and reduces accidental taps. Large readable font is intentional for aged users.

---

### Step 3 — Confirming a Medicine (Check Interaction)

- User taps the circular check button on a medicine card
- Animation: Checkmark fills the circle, medicine name gets a smooth strikethrough animation
- Card slides down and settles into a **"Taken"** section at the bottom of the screen
- Taken section is visually muted (lower opacity, lighter color) to keep focus on remaining medicines
- "Check All" button in a category marks all medicines in that group at once with a staggered animation

**Reasoning:** The strikethrough + move animation gives satisfying visual feedback that the action was registered. Moving taken medicines to the bottom keeps the active list clean and short — critical for aged users who can get overwhelmed by a long list.

---

### Step 4 — Tapping a Medicine Card (Detail Actions)

- Tapping anywhere on the card (except the check button) opens a bottom sheet
- Bottom sheet shows:
    - Medicine name and details at top
    - **Mark as Taken** — large primary button
    - **Snooze** — select snooze duration (15 min / 30 min / 1 hour) shown as 3 tap chips
    - **Skip** — with a confirmation prompt "Are you sure you want to skip this dose?"
    - **View Medicine Details** — secondary text link at bottom

**Reasoning:** Bottom sheet keeps the user on the home screen without a full page navigation. Three snooze options as tap chips instead of a time picker is faster and more accessible for aged users. Skip requires confirmation to prevent accidental skips which could affect health tracking accuracy.

---

### Step 5 — Missed State

- Medicines not confirmed past their scheduled time automatically move to a **"Missed"** section
- Missed cards are highlighted with a subtle red/amber left border
- User can still mark a missed medicine as taken (late confirmation) or skip it from the missed section
- Missed count shown as a small badge on the date in the date strip for past dates

**Reasoning:** Automatic missed detection removes the burden from the user. Allowing late confirmation is important — users may genuinely take medicine late and need to log it accurately. The red border is subtle enough to not feel alarming but visible enough to draw attention.

---

### Suggested Improvements

**For All Users**

- **Daily progress bar** — A thin progress bar below the date strip showing X of Y medicines taken today
- **Quick check from notification** — Allow users to mark medicine as taken directly from the push notification without opening the app

### Error States

- **No medicines scheduled for selected date** — Empty state illustration with message "No medicines scheduled for this day"
- **All medicines already taken** — Positive empty state "All done for today! 🎉" with a motivational line
- **Snooze limit reached** — If user snoozes same medicine more than 3 times, a gentle warning "You've snoozed this medicine multiple times. Please take it or skip it."
- **Past date selected** — Check button disabled on past date cards, only "View" mode available with a label "You are viewing a past date"

---

### Empty States

- **No medicines added yet** — Full canvas empty state with illustration, message "You haven't added any medicines yet" and a CTA "Add your first medicine"
- **Taken section empty** — Section simply hidden until at least one medicine is confirmed, does not show an empty placeholder
- **Missed section empty** — Section hidden entirely if no medicines are missed, not shown as an empty block

---

### Interaction Rules

- Check button is the only tap target on the card for confirmation, rest of card opens bottom sheet
- Check All confirms all pending medicines in that time group with a staggered animation
- Past dates are view only, no check or snooze actions allowed. A button allowed to edit that day.
- Missed state triggers automatically after scheduled time passes without confirmation
- Snooze reschedules the notification only, does not move the card position
- Skip requires a single confirmation tap, no lengthy dialog
- Taken section always stays at the bottom, below all active time groups
- Missed section appears between active groups and taken section
- Date strip only allows scrolling up to 30 days back and 30 days forward
- "Today" button only visible when user is not on current date
